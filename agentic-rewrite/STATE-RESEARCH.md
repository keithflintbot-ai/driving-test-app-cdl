# State Research Checklist

Before generating questions for a state, research these topics. Focus on **concepts and thresholds**, not exact numbers.

---

## GDL/Licensing (12 questions)

Research:
- Minimum age for learner's permit
- Minimum age for restricted/provisional license
- Minimum age for full unrestricted license
- Required holding period before road test
- Supervised driving hours required (if any)
- Nighttime driving restrictions (curfew)
- Passenger restrictions for new drivers
- Who can supervise (age, license requirements)

Question focus:
- Age thresholds and why they exist
- What restrictions apply and when they lift
- Consequences of violating GDL rules

---

## Cell Phone Laws (6 questions)

Research:
- Hands-free requirement (all drivers or just teens?)
- Texting ban (all drivers?)
- Exceptions (emergencies, GPS, parked?)
- Primary vs secondary enforcement
- Special rules for learners/provisional drivers

Question focus:
- What's allowed vs prohibited
- Who the laws apply to
- When exceptions apply

---

## DUI State Laws (8 questions)

Research:
- BAC limit for adults (usually 0.08%)
- Zero tolerance BAC for under 21
- Implied consent law details
- What happens if you refuse testing
- Administrative vs criminal process (general)
- Lookback period for prior offenses
- Ignition interlock requirements

Question focus:
- Understanding BAC limits and why
- Consequences of refusal
- How implied consent works

---

## Insurance (4 questions)

Research:
- At-fault or no-fault state?
- Is proof of insurance required in vehicle?
- What liability insurance covers
- Consequences of driving uninsured

Question focus:
- Understanding what insurance types cover
- Legal requirements (not dollar amounts)
- Consequences of non-compliance

---

## State Unique (10 questions)

Research the state's specific rules on:
- Move-over law (for emergency vehicles)
- Headlight requirements (when required)
- Right turn on red rules
- School bus stopping rules
- Motorcycle lane splitting (if legal)
- Speed limits in school/work zones
- Any unusual or unique local laws
- Winter driving requirements (chains, studded tires)
- HOV/carpool lane rules
- Wildlife crossing rules (if applicable)

Question focus:
- Rules that differ from other states
- Situations unique to that state's geography/climate
- Common mistakes drivers make in that state

---

## Research Tips

1. **Use official DMV sources** - State DMV websites and driver handbooks
2. **Check recent changes** - Laws update frequently, verify current rules
3. **Note what's unique** - Focus on how this state differs from others
4. **Avoid memorization traps** - Don't write questions about exact fines or points
